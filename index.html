Introduction

Quarks are elementary particles that form the building blocks of matter. They combine in various ways to create protons and neutrons, which are found in the nuclei of atoms. There are six types, or "flavors," of quarks: up, down, charm, strange, top, and bottom. Each type has its own properties and characteristics. Quarks are held together by the strong nuclear force, which is one of the four fundamental forces of nature. Their interactions are described by the theory of quantum chromodynamics. Without quarks, the universe as we know it wouldn't exist!
